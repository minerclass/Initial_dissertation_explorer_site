Major Wins and Gains

SEC

1.1 Cybersecurity, Governance, and Data Privacy

  • Security posture transformation: Google security score improved from 73% to 92%; Microsoft Secure Score maintained at 94%. LDAP signing was deployed, and RC4/DES was disabled on domain controllers.
  • National recognition: Earned the CoSN Trusted Learning Environment (TLE) Mini-Seal for Leadership; TLE Business Mini-Seal application submitted.
  • Funding secured: FCC Cybersecurity Pilot funding awarded at $27,500 per year for 3 years, supporting Sophos and SHI MDR/XDR and Check Point Harmony email security.
  • Staff training and MFA rollout: District-wide MFA implemented by early 2025; Cybernut phishing awareness and cybersecurity training launched.

NET

1.2 Network Infrastructure and Security

  • 10GB backbone upgrades: Major E-Rate Cat6 and fiber upgrades completed at Kenneth Murphy, Newport, and the District Office; Howe Elementary is scheduled for Summer 2026.
  • Firewall and filtering upgrades: Upgraded to Fortigate 401F and migrated filtering from Lightspeed Relay to Linewize (Qoria).
  • Bandwidth recovery: Student YouTube restrictions reclaimed more than 32% of network capacity for instructional tools such as i-Ready.
  • Physical security enhancements: Raptor self-check-in kiosks installed, unified staff ID/FOB badges rolled out, IP-based Valcom paging updated, and vape detectors piloted at the middle school.

CLS

1.3 Classroom Innovation and Asset Management

  • Classroom modernization: 40+ Newline Q-Series/Q Pro interactive displays and 60+ Vivi wireless screen mirroring devices deployed, replacing outdated Epson projectors.
  • 1:1 device deployment and recovery: 2,100 student Chromebooks and 275 teacher laptops deployed for 2025–2026; Senturo added for geolocation and remote locking to recover lost devices.
  • Interactive learning environments: Lü System installed in the Howe Elementary and Kenneth Murphy gyms.
  • AI integration: MagicSchool AI and Google Gemini licenses rolled out for teachers and administrators.

Notable Challenges

RISK

2.1 External Vendor Breaches and Software Disruptions

  • PowerSchool data breach (Dec. 2024 / Jan. 2025): Managed communications and community response after the third-party breach; coordinated with neighboring districts to provide credit monitoring to affected families.
  • Lightspeed agent outage (Oct. 2025): Residual software from decommissioned Lightspeed servers blocked internet access on devices, requiring urgent policy updates and agent removal.
!Vendor risks
AUD

2.2 Audit Findings and Documentation Gaps

  • Secure Halo cybersecurity review (Nov. 2025): Overall Grade C (73%), above peer average, but Grade D gaps identified in Data Recovery, Data Protection, and Vulnerability Management.
  • Response: Prioritized formal Incident Response Plan updates and established a data recovery testing schedule.
CGrade C (73%) Critical gaps: Grade D
HW

2.3 Rising Hardware Costs and Physical Infrastructure Limits

  • Fiscal sustainability of 1:1 devices: Rising Chromebook costs made the 3-year refresh cycle unsustainable, leading to a shift from a K/3/6 cycle to a K/6 cycle to maximize the 8-year Auto-Update Expiration (AUE).
  • Legacy cabling and structural hurdles: Broken conduits delayed the Oak Crest Bus Depot camera project, and cabling limitations at Howe Elementary restricted the south hall to 1GB instead of 10GB.

Overall Story

A period of major growth in cybersecurity, infrastructure, classroom modernization, and instructional technology, alongside important lessons in vendor risk, documentation, fiscal sustainability, and legacy infrastructure limitations.
